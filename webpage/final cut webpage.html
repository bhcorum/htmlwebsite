My List of Hobbies

First and foremost, I enjoy playing video games.

I've fallen in love with the Souls' games. Dark Souls 3 is the game that got me into this style of games. This game is a descendant of the infamous Dark Souls game. While this game is a little easier than Dark Souls, it shares in core gameplay elements in a sense that they both take place in what we would call, the Medieval Period. The game makes you constantly think about how you're going to approach every new area. With "one-shotting" enemies and map hazards, dying and restarting is a huge part of this game. In fact the original Dark Souls was remade with the title of "Dark Souls: Prepare to Die Edition".

The cycle goes like this:
  1. Fight a Boss
  2. Die to said Boss
  3. Grind souls on trash mob enemies
  4. Return to the Firelink Shrine
  5. Upgrade your skills
  6. Return to your last visited bonfire
  7. Progress to Boss area
  8. Beat the Boss and proceed
  9. Repeat

Sekiro is another game in this series that, unlike Dark Souls, is set in Japan and focuses more on clashing swords with your opponent no matter how big they are. This game is equally focused on mystical superpowers and like Dark Souls, you are on a mission to link your life-force with its origin. Between fighting katana wielding lackeys, or fighting mystical divine beasts, this game pulls even less punches than Dark Souls 3. Without being able to summon friends to assist you, you're truly alone in the world on a quest to follow the Iron Code and help your new-found master... or will you?
